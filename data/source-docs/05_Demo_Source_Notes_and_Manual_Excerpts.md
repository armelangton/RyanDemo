# Demo Source Notes and Manual Excerpts

These are fictionalized demo notes created to help the app demonstrate how AI can combine recall data with internal site/product context.

Do not present these as official manufacturer instructions or official Ryan Fire Protection documentation.

## Extinguisher Training Demo Note
A facility team has requested a refresher on extinguisher readiness and basic visual checks.
Internal prep notes:
- Confirm extinguisher placement and accessibility.
- Review inspection tags and service date visibility.
- Remind the audience that damaged, missing, discharged, or blocked extinguishers should be reported.
- Include a plain-language explanation of when staff should evacuate instead of attempting to use an extinguisher.
- Verify official training and safety procedures before instruction.
Possible audience questions:
- How often should staff visually check extinguishers?
- What should be reported immediately?
- What should someone do if an extinguisher was used or partially discharged?
- What if the extinguisher looks damaged or the tag is missing?

## Fire Alarm / Detection Demo Note
A customer site has alarm panel and detection equipment in its service profile.
Internal prep notes:
- Confirm panel documentation and test history.
- Verify whether any affected product models appear in the site equipment list.
- Confirm whether alarm testing records are current.
- Prepare a plain-language explanation of what the customer should document and report.
- Review official manufacturer guidance before advising on specific equipment actions.
Possible audience questions:
- What records should be kept after testing?
- What should staff do if an alarm device is damaged or missing?
- How should issues be escalated?

## Sprinkler / Fire Pump Demo Note
A fire department recruit training session includes sprinkler system behavior and fire pump overview.
Internal prep notes:
- Explain the difference between system awareness and system operation authority.
- Highlight why responders should recognize control valves, sprinkler activation, and waterflow indicators.
- Include discussion around what should be reported after an incident.
- Avoid giving site-specific operational instructions unless authorized by qualified personnel.
- Verify training points against official procedures and qualified instructor review.

## Special Hazards Demo Note
An industrial account has special hazard suppression equipment.
Internal prep notes:
- Treat system-specific details as higher risk and requiring qualified review.
- Verify manufacturer documentation before discussing technical actions.
- Confirm exact equipment type, model, service history, and inspection status.
- Include a clear human review reminder.
- If foam-related context is involved, avoid unsupported PFAS or environmental claims; state what needs qualified review.
