# Ryan Service Lens Guide

Use this guide to make AI outputs more specific to Ryan Fire Protection’s service world.

## Inspection, Testing & Maintenance
Focus on inspection timing, testing cadence, maintenance needs, service history, deficiencies, documentation, upcoming service needs, customer portal/report visibility, and what should be verified before action.

## Customer Training
Focus on plain-language education, materials to bring, likely attendee questions, demonstration points, customer confidence, what staff should recognize and report, and follow-up reminders.

## Fire Sprinklers
Focus on sprinkler system awareness, valves, waterflow, wet/dry/preaction context when relevant, inspection and testing reminders, documentation, system behavior during response, and service history.

## Fire Alarm & Detection
Focus on alarm testing, panel awareness, detection devices, monitoring/notification context, customer communication, documentation and service records, and recurring inspection/testing reminders.

## Fire Extinguishers
Focus on extinguisher readiness, inspection tags, placement, monthly visual checks, employee training, replacement/remedy verification, and documentation.

## Fire Hydrants
Focus on inspection, flow testing, public safety, municipal coordination, documentation, access/visibility, and recurring reminders.

## Special Hazards
Focus on higher-risk equipment or environments, system-specific documentation, manufacturer guidance, qualified internal review, specialized inspection/testing needs, customer communication, and emergency response awareness.

## Emergency Service
Focus on urgency, triage, what to verify immediately, who may need notification, what documentation/photos/notes are needed, and immediate follow-up.

## Clean Foam Testing
Focus on foam system testing awareness, environmental/public health sensitivity, documentation, qualified review, and PFAS-related caution without making unsupported claims.

## Documentation / Deficiency Follow-Up
Focus on inspection notes, photos, reports, deficiency verification, customer communication, quote preparation, approval/follow-up, and documentation needed for state/local officials or customer records.

## Output behavior

Every AI output should reflect the selected service lens. If the selected service lens does not clearly apply to the recall/product, the assistant should say what needs to be verified rather than forcing a connection.
